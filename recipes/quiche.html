The greatest Quiche Lodin

Ingredients

For the shortcrust pastry

  • 250 g of flour
  • 125 g of cold butter
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons of ice water

For the filling

  • 200 g smoked bacon
  • 500 ml heavy cream
  • 3 eggs
  • 150 g Emmental cheese
  • Salt
  • White pepper
  • Nutmeg

Steps

For the dough

  1. In a large bowl, combine 250 g of wheat flour with 125 g of cold butter, cut into small cubes, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Using your fingertips, work the ingredients until they have a sandy texture.
  3. Add 1 egg yolk and 2 tablespoons of ice-cold water.
  4. Mix the ingredients just until they come together, without overworking the dough. It should easily detach from the bowl.
  5. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  6. After chilling, remove the dough from the fridge, roll it out with a rolling pin, and place it into a greased round mold (about 24 cm in diameter).
  7. Line the dough with parchment paper and place weights on top (such as dry legumes or ceramic baking beads) to prevent it from puffing up.
  8. Preheat the oven to 180°C (356°F) with top and bottom heat and bake the crust for 10 minutes

For the filling

  1. Cut 200 g of smoked bacon into evenly sized cubes.
  2. Sauté the bacon in a pan over medium-high heat until golden brown.
  3. In a bowl, beat 500 ml of cooking cream with 3 eggs.
  4. Grate 150 g of Emmental cheese and mix it with the eggs and cream.
  5. Season the mixture with salt, white pepper, and freshly grated nutmeg.
  6. Remove the crust from the oven, take out the weights and parchment paper, and pour the filling into the pastry shell.
  7. Bake the quiche at 180°C (356°F) for 30 minutes with top and bottom heat.
  8. Once baked, let it cool slightly before removing it from the mold.
  9. Serve warm or cold, even better the next day!
